About Me:

The who, what, where, when, how, and sometimes why.

Contact Info Download Resume

My interest in technology began with a straightforward conviction: exceptional IT is not about fixing problems — it is about engineering systems resilient enough to prevent them.

Over seven years of progressive technical experience in the greater Portland, Maine area, I have built a career at the intersection of enterprise systems administration and modern software engineering. Today I operate as a Core Tier 3 NOC Engineer and informal Site Reliability Engineer at Systems Engineering, a Managed Service Provider responsible for the technical operations of over 253 multi-sector client organizations. My daily focus centers on automated infrastructure design, critical incident response, and cloud engineering.

Current projects include an end-to-end SSL certificate automation engine built on Win-Acme and Let's Encrypt, validated across Azure DNS and Cloudflare in a sandbox environment I designed and deployed personally. I also serve on the corporate AI Task Force, contributing engineering direction for emerging technology integration. In parallel, I am completing a Bachelor of Science in Software Engineering at Western Governors University, with an expected graduation date of December 2026.

Throughout my career I have been recognized with multiple professional awards — including the Systems Engineering Corporate Shooting Star Award, the Outstanding Employee Owner of the Year nomination, and the Spectrum Healthcare Q4 Kineo Award — each a reflection of a standard I hold to regardless of formal recognition.

My GitHub documents active project work. For professional opportunities or questions, I can be reached at julian.lall@outlook.com.
